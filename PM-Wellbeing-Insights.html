The Pressure on Project Professionals

A data-driven snapshot of the wellbeing challenge in project management.

The Wellbeing Challenge

50%

of PMs experience burnout, the highest rate of any profession surveyed. [1]

72%

report their main project has negatively impacted their mental wellbeing. [2, 3]

40%

suffer from prolonged, chronic stress in their roles. [1]

85%

of PMs facing organizational hurdles burn out from unrealistic deadlines. [5]

The Ripple Effect

37%

of PMs have considered leaving the profession due to pressure. [5, 6]

27%

Manager engagement level, a historic low, down from 30%. [4]

$8.9T

Global economic cost of low employee engagement (9% of GDP). [4, 17]

The Key Drivers

Workload & Deadlines

  • 85% of PMs manage multiple projects concurrently. [5, 6, 18]
  • 54% of all employees cite workload as their top stressor. [15]
  • 70% more likely to burn out from unreasonable time constraints. [20]

The Resource & Leadership Gap

  • 44% of PMs cite "not enough resources" as a top challenge. [5, 6]
  • Perception Gap: 43% of PMs feel teams are understaffed, but only 20% of C-suite executives agree. [5]
  • A manager's behavior drives 70% of the variance in team engagement. [4]

The Path to Resilience: Strong Foundations

The Success Dividend

  • 92% success rate for organizations with mature PM practices. [6]
  • These organizations waste 28 times less money. [6]

The Wellbeing Dividend

  • After a successful project, 54% of employees experience less stress. [5]
  • 49% feel more in control at work. [5]

The Power of Psychological Safety

Workers in high-safety environments are far less likely to feel stressed (27% vs. 61%) or look for a new job (19% vs. 41%). [58]

The Future Outlook

29%

of project professionals feel unready for the adoption of AI tools. [62]

53%

of agile teams report experiencing burnout at some point. [63]
